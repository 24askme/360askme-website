2. Company Registration — Step by Step

Rwanda has one of the most efficient company registration systems in the world — and the government charges nothing for the online application. Here is exactly how it works.

Step 1: Reserve Your Company Name

Search the RDB online portal to ensure your desired company name is available. You can reserve it immediately — at no cost.

Step 2: Prepare Required Documents

  • Copy of passport (for foreign directors/shareholders)
  • Passport photos
  • Proof of address (utility bill or bank statement)
  • Memorandum and Articles of Association (template available online)

Step 3: Submit Online Application

All applications go through the RDB Company Registration Portal. The government fee is zero — completely free for online registration.

Step 4: Receive Certificate of Incorporation

Once approved (usually within 6-24 hours), you receive your Certificate of Incorporation, Tax Identification Number (TIN), and registration with RSSB (social security) automatically.

Step 5: Open a Corporate Bank Account

With your Certificate of Incorporation, you can open a business bank account with any local bank. 360ASKME can accompany you or handle the process remotely.

3. Taxes Every Business Owner Should Know

Rwanda's tax system is straightforward, digital, and transparent. The Rwanda Revenue Authority (RRA) has made filing and payment easy.

Key Taxes

  • Corporate Income Tax (CIT): 30% of net profits (reduced to 20% for certain sectors or startups in special economic zones)
  • Value Added Tax (VAT): 18% on most goods and services (registration required if annual turnover exceeds 20 million RWF)
  • Withholding Tax: 15% on dividends, interest, and certain payments
  • Pay As You Earn (PAYE): Progressive rates from 0% to 30% on employee salaries
  • Rental Income Tax: 20% on rental income

Tax Incentives for Investors

  • Tax holidays for qualifying investments (up to 7 years)
  • Reduced corporate tax rates (15% for certain sectors)
  • VAT exemptions on qualifying capital equipment
  • Duty-free import of machinery and raw materials

4. Business Permits & Licenses

Depending on your industry, you may need additional permits beyond the Certificate of Incorporation.

Common Permits

  • Operational License (City of Kigali): Required for physical businesses (restaurants, shops, offices)
  • Sector-Specific Licenses: Health (pharmacies, clinics), Education (schools), Tourism (hotels, tour operators), Construction
  • Import/Export License: For businesses trading across borders
  • Work Permit: For foreign employees (including the business owner)

Work Permits for Business Owners

If you are a foreigner starting a business in Rwanda, you will need a work permit. Options include:

  • Investor Visa: For those investing at least $50,000 (reduced for certain sectors)
  • Employment Work Permit: For those employed by their own company
  • Long-Term Visa (Business): For frequent business travelers

6. Foreign Investment & Ownership Rules

Rwanda is one of the most open economies in Africa for foreign investors — truly the best investment destination in Africa for those seeking ease and transparency.

Key Rules

  • 100% foreign ownership is permitted in most sectors
  • No local partner requirement for company registration
  • Repatriation of profits is allowed without restriction
  • Investment protection under Rwandan law and bilateral treaties
  • Minimum investment threshold for Investor Visa is $50,000 (lower for certain sectors)

Restricted Sectors (Require Approval)

  • Media (broadcasting licenses)
  • Security services
  • Mining (mineral rights)
  • Telecommunications

8. Hiring Employees in Rwanda

Rwanda has a young, educated, English-speaking workforce. Here is what you need to know about building your team.

Key Employment Considerations

  • Minimum wage: There is no national minimum wage. 360ASKME helps you set competitive, fair salaries that attract top talent while respecting market conditions.
  • Working hours: 40 hours per week (8 hours/day, 5 days/week)
  • Paid leave: 18 working days per year minimum
  • Social security (RSSB): Mandatory contributions — 8% employer, 4% employee
  • Probation period: Maximum 3 months (6 months for specialized roles)
  • Termination notice: 1-3 months depending on length of service
